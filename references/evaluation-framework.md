# Evaluation Framework for Game Ideas

## Idea Selection Criteria

Before scoring, verify each candidate idea meets these minimum criteria:

- **Pain point driven**: Addresses a pain point or unmet desire expressed by real users in the research sources
- **Feasible scope**: Buildable by a team of 1–3 developers without AAA-level resources
- **Revenue path**: Has a plausible path to revenue on mobile or Steam (or both)

Discard ideas that fail any of these before applying the scoring rubric below.

---

## Scoring Rubric

Rate each game idea across five dimensions on a scale of **1–5**. Be honest — a lower score isn't a disqualifier, it's information.

---

## 1. 実現可能性 (Feasibility) — Can a small team actually build this?

Focus on technical complexity, required assets, and scope.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Simple mechanics, minimal assets needed, buildable solo in under 6 months |
| 4 | Moderate complexity, manageable with 2–3 people, standard tech stack |
| 3 | Requires specialized skills or moderate asset volume, but achievable |
| 2 | High complexity or large asset requirements — risk of scope creep |
| 1 | Requires AAA resources, proprietary tech, or a large team |

---

## 2. 開発期間 (Development Period) — How quickly can it ship?

**Shorter = higher score.** Speed to market matters for small teams with limited runway.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | MVP shippable in 1–3 months |
| 4 | MVP in 3–6 months |
| 3 | 6–12 months to a releasable version |
| 2 | 12–18 months |
| 1 | 18+ months or indefinite scope |

---

## 3. 収益性 (Profitability) — Can it make money?

Consider monetization model, market size, and willingness to pay.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Clear monetization (paid app, premium + DLC, subscription), demonstrated willingness to pay in the niche |
| 4 | Solid monetization path, reasonably sized market |
| 3 | Monetization possible but requires careful execution (e.g., F2P with IAP that could alienate) |
| 2 | Niche audience or unproven willingness to pay |
| 1 | Likely to attract players but very hard to convert to revenue |

---

## 4. 競合優位性 (Competitive Advantage) — Is the niche truly underserved?

How much room is there to win?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | No direct competitors; clear gap in the market |
| 4 | A few competitors with significant flaws or outdated titles |
| 3 | Some competition, but opportunity to differentiate meaningfully |
| 2 | Crowded space; winning requires exceptional execution |
| 1 | Dominated by established titles with loyal fanbases |

---

## 5. 小規模開発適性 (Small-Team Suitability) — Is this concept suited to a small indie team?

Some games only work at scale. This dimension asks whether the idea plays to a small team's strengths.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Thrives with a focused vision and indie charm; no economies of scale needed |
| 4 | Works well for small teams; maybe benefits from polish over time |
| 3 | Feasible but requires external help (e.g., freelance art, music licensing) |
| 2 | Concept requires breadth that strains small teams (large map, many systems) |
| 1 | Fundamentally requires a large team to deliver on the promise |

---

## Scoring Summary

After rating all five dimensions, compute:

- **Total Score**: sum of all five ratings (max 25)
- **Overall Grade**:
  - 20–25: Excellent — strong candidate
  - 14–19: Good — worth pursuing with caveats
  - 8–13: Moderate — significant risks to address
  - Below 8: Weak — probably pass

Present scores in a table alongside the overall grade and a one-line rationale for each score.
